Porter Governor Problem

A Porter governor is a type of centrifugal governor used to control the speed of engines. It consists of two rotating balls connected to a sleeve through a system of arms. The governor operates by balancing the centrifugal force of the rotating balls against the weight of the central load and sleeve friction.

Given Parameters:
  • Central load mass = {{ central_mass }} kg
  • Mass of each ball = {{ ball_mass }} kg
  • Length of top arms = {{ top_arms }} mm
  • Length of bottom arms = {{ bottom_arms }} mm
  • Sleeve friction force = {{ sleeve_friction }} N
  • Inclination angle of upper arms with vertical = {{ arms_angle }}°
Problem Statement:

For the given Porter governor configuration, determine the range of speed (in rpm) within which the governor can maintain equilibrium. The range represents the difference between the maximum and minimum speeds at which the governor operates effectively.

Note: Consider the effects of sleeve friction and the geometry of the governor mechanism in your calculations.
